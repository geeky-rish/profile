How I Think
About Building

RK
Open to work

I obsess over the problem first

Before writing a single line of code, I ask: does this actually need to exist? I care about shipping things that solve real friction — not things that look impressive in a demo but die in production.

I ship, then iterate

I believe in momentum over perfection. Get a working version in front of users fast, gather real signal, then refine. Most engineers spend months polishing; I prefer to spend weeks learning.

I care about the seams

AI can generate code. What matters is the architectural judgment — knowing where state lives, where failures cascade, how to keep the system debuggable six months from now. That's the craft I'm building.
